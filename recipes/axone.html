Axone (Assam and Nagaland)

Description

Axone, also known as akhuni, is a cherished fermented soybean dish from Nagaland, India, deeply rooted in the culinary traditions of the Sumi Naga tribe. Renowned for its pungent aroma and distinctive umami flavor, it’s a versatile ingredient used as both a condiment and a base in various dishes, including curries, chutneys, and pickles. The preparation involves boiling soybeans until tender, draining them, and fermenting them in bamboo baskets or wrapped in banana leaves near a fire for several days, often followed by gentle pounding to create a semi-coarse paste or cakes. This process imparts a smoky, bitter undertone that elevates dishes like smoked pork curry, vegetable stews, or spicy king chili chutneys, making axone a bold, acquired taste that embodies Nagaland’s rich food culture.

Ingredients for Axone (Akhuni) Preparation

  • 500 grams whole soybeans
  • Water (for soaking and boiling)
  • Optional: Banana leaves or bamboo baskets (for fermentation)

Ingredients for a Basic Axone Pork Curry

  • 2 tablespoons axone (fermented soybean paste or cake)
  • 500 grams pork (preferably with fat, cut into cubes)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies or 1-2 king chilies (bhut jolokia), slit (adjust to taste)
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated or minced
  • 4-5 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 medium tomato, chopped (optional)
  • 1-2 teaspoons turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons mustard oil or vegetable oil
  • 1-2 cups water (adjust for desired consistency)
  • Optional: Fresh coriander leaves or Naga herbs for garnish

Note: Axone’s strong flavor means a little goes a long way. Adjust quantities based on preference, and ensure proper ventilation when cooking due to its pungent aroma.

Steps to Prepare Axone (Akhuni)

  1. Soak Soybeans: Rinse 500 grams of soybeans thoroughly and soak them in water for 8-12 hours or overnight.
  2. Boil Soybeans: Drain the soaked soybeans, place them in a large pot, and cover with fresh water. Boil for 2-3 hours until soft but not mushy. Drain and let cool slightly.
  3. Ferment Soybeans: Transfer the boiled soybeans to a clean bamboo basket or wrap them tightly in banana leaves. Place near a low heat source (like a fireplace) or in a warm, dark place for 4-7 days to ferment. Check for a strong, pungent smell and sticky texture.
  4. Process Axone: Once fermented, lightly pound the soybeans into a coarse paste or form into small cakes. Store in an airtight container for use.

Steps to Cook Axone Pork Curry

  1. Prepare Ingredients: Measure out 2 tablespoons of axone, chop 500 grams of pork into cubes, and finely chop 1 onion, 1 tomato (if using), 2-3 green chilies or 1-2 king chilies, 1-inch ginger, and 4-5 garlic cloves.
  2. Heat Oil: In a heavy-bottomed pot, heat 2 tablespoons of mustard oil over medium heat until it smokes lightly. Reduce heat slightly.
  3. Sauté Aromatics: Add chopped onion, ginger, and garlic. Sauté for 3-4 minutes until golden and fragrant.
  4. Add Axone: Stir in 2 tablespoons of axone paste or crumbled cake. Cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly to release its aroma (use ventilation due to the strong smell).
  5. Cook Pork: Add pork cubes and 1-2 teaspoons of turmeric powder. Stir well to coat the meat with the axone mixture. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the pork starts to brown.
  6. Add Chilies and Tomato: Add slit chilies and chopped tomato (if using). Cook for another 2-3 minutes until the tomato softens.
  7. Simmer Curry: Pour in 1-2 cups of water, depending on desired consistency. Add salt to taste. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the pork is tender and the flavors meld.
  8. Check Consistency: If the curry is too thick, add a bit more water; if too thin, simmer uncovered for a few more minutes.
  9. Garnish and Serve: Optionally, garnish with fresh coriander or Naga herbs. Serve hot with steamed rice or sticky rice.

Note: Cook in a well-ventilated area due to axone’s pungent odor. Adjust chili and axone quantities to suit your taste.
